Diabetes Treatment and How often you should Visit the Doctor

Since diabetes is seen as a high sugar levels, the best method of medication is to keep one's sugar levels at a satisfactory level. Consultation with a medical doctor is imperative to gain a comprehension on the sugar levels are acceptable.

Insulin Injections

nsulin is mainly categorized into short acting, intermediate acting and long acting insulin.Short acting insulins start their work within minutes of being injected but they do not last long.Long acting insulin takes time to begin its action but lasts long. Hence, Short acting insulin can control the sugar surge that happens immediately after a meal whereas long acting insulin will maintain blood sugars over the day.The current trend however is to give insulin combinations where a certain percentage of insulin is short acting whereas the rest is long acting. This is helpful in controlling both post prandial sugar surge and over the day maintainence.

Alpha-glucosidase inhibitors

regulates the absorption of sugar into your bloodstream.

Sulfonylurea drugs

stimulates the natural manufacture of insulin within the body

Thiazolidinediones

Causes body tissues in becoming more responsive to insulin

Biguanides

These reduce the glucose production in the liver

Gliptins

There is a certain hormone called incretin which regulates insulin secretion and glucose production. An enzyme, called DPP-4 destroys this hormone. This group of drugs block the destruction of incretin, hence helping the Maintainence of insulin secretion.

How often should we visit the doctor

Now you would like to know if your daily efforts at controlling your Blood Glucose are paying off or keeping other health problems away. You can find your answer by keeping in touch with your Doctor and taking regular blood check-ups.

The frequency of medical visits varies according to how long a person is being diagnosed as Diabetic, his /her ability to maintain good blood glucose control and whether he/ she have any diabetic complications or other medical conditions.

Maintaining your blood glucose tells your Doctor whether the current treatment is controlling your diabetes well or not and it can also prevent or reduce your hypoglycaemic episodes.

The American Diabetes Association (ADA) recommends quarterly appointments for people who take Insulin or Uncontrolled blood sugar levels and two to three annual visits for rest of the diabetics. The researchers suggests that treatment could be more efficient with frequent visits which will lead to better control.
